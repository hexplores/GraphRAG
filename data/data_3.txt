Title: Travel Guide – Kyoto

Kyoto, once the capital of Japan, is famous for its temples, shrines, and traditional wooden houses.

Top attractions include:
- Fushimi Inari Shrine
- Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion)
- Arashiyama Bamboo Forest

Kyoto is especially popular during cherry blossom season in spring and autumn foliage season in November.

Local cuisine highlights include kaiseki dining, yudofu (tofu hot pot), and matcha-based sweets. Nishiki Market is a popular place to sample regional foods.

Getting around is easiest via buses and the subway. A day pass can be cost-effective if you plan multiple rides.

Respectful etiquette is important at temples and shrines. Visitors should be quiet, avoid touching sacred objects, and follow posted guidance.

Day trips from Kyoto include Nara for its deer park and temples, and Uji for tea farms and the Byodo-in Temple.

Early mornings are quieter at major sites like Fushimi Inari and Kiyomizu-dera, making them ideal for photos.

Gion is known for its traditional streets and teahouses. Evening walks can be a highlight.

Many gardens, such as Ryoan-ji, feature zen rock layouts designed for contemplation.
